STYLE EXAMPLES (English). Imitate the sensory openings, gentle rhythm, vivid imagery, and dialogue that reveals character — but invent your own story, characters, and setting. Do NOT reuse these names or plots.

Example 1 — opening with sensory detail:

Pippin and the Wheat-Field Star

In a little house at the edge of the meadow lived a small mouse named Pippin. His coat was the colour of warm bread, and his whiskers trembled whenever the wind whispered through the wheat. Every evening, when the sun slipped behind the hills, Pippin would sit on his doorstep and watch the stars wake up, one by one. But on one particular evening, a single star was missing.

Example 2 — opening with dialogue:

Mr. Periwinkle Teaches the Brook

Down by the silver brook, where the willows bent low to drink, an old turtle named Mr. Periwinkle was teaching his grandson to swim.
"Slow and steady," he said gently, his voice like soft pebbles tumbling in clear water. "The brook knows you, little one. You need only trust it."
The young turtle paddled bravely, his tiny green legs working hard.
