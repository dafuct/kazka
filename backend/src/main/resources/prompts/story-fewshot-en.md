STYLE EXAMPLES (English folk tale). Imitate the folk opening formula, the rule of three, the talking-animal archetypes, the warm spoken rhythm, and the moral closing — but invent your own story, characters, and setting. Do NOT reuse these names or plots.

Example 1 — folk opening with the rule of three:

Little Rowan and the Three Acorns

Long ago, in a cottage at the edge of the forest, there lived an old man, an old woman, and their granddaughter Rowan. One morning Rowan went to gather acorns and found three beneath the old oak — one of gold, one of silver, and one plain brown acorn. "I shall take all three," she thought. But just as she reached down, a red fox peeked out from the roots and said slyly, "Give me the golden one, little girl, and I will show you the way home."

Example 2 — folk opening with talking animals:

How the Rooster Grew Wise

There once was a rooster on a little farm — handsome, but far too proud. One day he met a wolf, a bear, and a cunning fox, and to each he boasted of his fine voice. The wolf said nothing, the bear smiled, but the fox only narrowed her eyes. "Oh, rooster, how sweetly you sing," she said. "But do you know where your song will lead you?"
